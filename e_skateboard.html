Overview

I started this project, because I wanted to get myself a electric skateboard, but I always thought that having a remote in one hand is inconvenient. So I wanted to make a electric skateboard that works without a remote. There were multiple options for that. I decided to go with strain sensors. Strain sensors are mounted to both skateboard trucks. That allows me to measure the strain in both truck and as well calculate weight that is applied to both trucks. I made a program, that calculates the difference between those weights and according to that sets the motor speed.

Struggles

Mounting the pulley

It should have been a easy task, but I didn't have all the right tools for the job and this made it very difficult. Difficult part was drilling straight holes through the wheel. I had to do it with a hand drill. Turns out, that it wasn't a goo idea. Hand drilled holes weren't straight and I had to make those wider, so that the bolts could go through the holes straight.

Installing the strain gauges

Gluing stain gauges to the truck was an easy part, but soldering the wires to the strain gauges was really hard. Those strain gauge solder pads were very small and it made it difficult to get the wire right on top of the solder pad. I did destroy few of the strain gauges before I was able to wire everything up correctly.
